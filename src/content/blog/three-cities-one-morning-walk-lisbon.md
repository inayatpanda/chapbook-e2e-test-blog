I have a habit I'm not sorry about: in a new city, I get up before it does. The same plan everywhere — no map, no agenda, just an hour on foot before breakfast while the place is still itself and not yet a performance for visitors. This is the first of three of those walks. We start in Lisbon.

## Lisbon makes you earn the view

Lisbon is built on hills the way other cities are built on grids, and a morning walk here is really a series of small climbs, each one paid off with a *miradouro* — a terrace flung open over a tumble of terracotta roofs to the river beyond. Go early and you get them almost to yourself, save for an old man, a coffee, and a view he has presumably seen ten thousand times and still turns up for.

The city is tiled like a bathroom and somehow it's beautiful. *Azulejos* — blue-and-white ceramic tiles — climb the fronts of the buildings, and in the low morning light they do a quiet trick: the whole street glows faintly, as though lit from inside. A tram grinds past in the yellow of a child's drawing. Somewhere a bakery vents the smell of warm *pastéis de nata*, and you abandon whatever plan you thought you had.

By nine the hills will be full of people pretending they aren't out of breath. For now it's just you, the gulls, and a city that wakes slowly and beautifully, in no hurry to impress anyone.

Next: a city that does mornings very differently — Kyoto, where the trick is to beat not the heat but the silence.
